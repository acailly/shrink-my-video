What does it do?

It applies some techniques to reduce the size of your video:


Convert to low video quality (480p)

You won't see tiny details but the essential is there

Convert to low audio quality (56 kbits/s)

Many videos remain audible with this settings

One image every 5 seconds (!)

For some videos, like conferences, there is not much action and you can easily understand what happens even with one image every 5 seconds

Why this tool?

TL;DR; to help reduce the environmental footprint of the digital world

This tool aim to humbly participate in stopping the race for ever more quality and bandwidth (8K resolution, 5G network, etc.) by offering a simple way to radically reduce the size of a video.

The digital world’s contribution to the footprint of humanity is currently around 4% (see this report from GreenIT.fr and this another report from The Shift Project). This is more than the aviation, and it is predicted to grow in the future.

Online video does NOT represent the main impact of the digital world, manufacturing is a much bigger deal. And I think reducing the size of a video can help keeping our old devices that have limited bandwidth and limited computing power, instead of buying new ones.

How it works?

Let's get into the technical details

This tool uses FFMPEG.WASM to edit the video directly in the browser!

You can dowload FFMPEG to try these settings directly on your computer

Convert to low video quality (480p)

ffmpeg -i input.mp4 -s hd480 output.mp4

Convert to low audio quality (56 kbits/s)

ffmpeg -i input.mp4 -c:a aac -b:a 56k output.mp4

One image every 5 seconds

ffmpeg -i input.mp4 -filter:v fps=fps=1/5 output.mp4

This approach is inspired by image dithering (see here for an example), which removes a lot of data in the image while preserving its essential meaning
